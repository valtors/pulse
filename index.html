your ai forgot everything. now it doesn't.

most ai lives in the cloud and forgets you exist the moment you close the tab. pulse is different. it runs on your machine. it connects to your accounts. it remembers. next time you ask, it knows what you meant last time.

$ pulse connect github <token> $ pulse ask "what did i miss" $ pulse remember focus "ship v1" $ pulse serve # web ui on :9090

what it is

a personal ai agent that lives on your machine. it connects to your accounts, stores what matters in sqlite, and uses any openai-compatible llm to reason about your data. no cloud. no dashboard. a binary.

memory

sqlite on disk. survives restarts. it remembers your focus, your projects, your context. you tell it something once, it knows forever. or until you tell it to forget.

connectors

github is wired. gmail and calendar are next. each connector feeds the same pipeline. add a service, pulse learns from it. remove one, pulse stops caring.

llm

bring your own key. any openai-compatible provider. without an llm, pulse does pattern matching. with one, it reasons about your notifications, answers questions, and writes summaries in your context.

local

your data never leaves your machine. no telemetry. no phone home. no account to create. sqlite on disk, config in json, one binary. that's it.

two languages. one binary.

rust

go calls rust. they share sqlite and json. no grpc, no protobuf, no microservices. two languages because each does what it's best at. if you can't tell why that's interesting, pulse isn't for you.

why

every notification tool is the same. inbox that shows everything. timeline that shows more. unread count that goes up. none of them know what you actually care about. they just show you more. pulse shows you less. the right less.

and it's yours. not a saas. not a freemium tier. not a data pipeline to someone's dashboard. your agent, your data, your machine.
